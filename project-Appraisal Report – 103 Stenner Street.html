Appraisal Report – 103 Stenner Street

Project Overview

This project was completed as part of my coursework for the AGB 376 Rural Property Appraisal class at California Polytechnic State University. The goal was to conduct a comprehensive appraisal of a multi-unit residential property located at 103 Stenner Street in San Luis Obispo, CA. I employed three key valuation methods: the cost approach, income approach, and sales comparison approach. Through detailed analysis, I developed a final market value estimate of $2,100,000, earning an A for this project.

Appraisal Details

Executive Summary

The property at 103 Stenner Street is a nine-bedroom, nine-bathroom apartment house located in a student-dense neighborhood near California Polytechnic State University. In this project, I analyzed the property’s value using standard appraisal techniques, which involved reviewing its physical condition, market potential, and zoning regulations. I also considered its rental income potential, the surrounding real estate market, and replacement costs. Ultimately, the appraisal concluded with a market value of $2,100,000 as of May 1, 2025. The analysis highlighted both the strong rental demand in the area and potential risks related to cash flow and competition.
